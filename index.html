Layers of the Skin

Skin (Overall) ▼

The skin is the largest organ of the body. It protects the body, regulates temperature, prevents water loss, and provides sensation.

Quiz: True or False: The skin is the largest organ in the body.

Epidermis ▼

Stratum Corneum ▼

Stage in shedding: Final stage, cells are dead and keratinized.

Structures: Flattened keratinocytes

Functions: Barrier against pathogens, prevents water loss

Quiz: True or False: These cells are no longer alive.

Stratum Lucidum ▼

Stage in shedding: Transition layer, clear cells.

Structures: Transparent keratinocytes

Functions: Extra protection in thick skin (palms/soles)

Quiz: Where is this layer found?

Stratum Granulosum ▼

Stage in shedding: Cells start dying, keratin accumulates.

Structures: Granular keratinocytes

Functions: Waterproofing, forms skin barrier

Quiz: True or False: Cells in this layer are still alive.

Stratum Spinosum ▼

Stage in shedding: Living cells, start producing keratin.

Structures: Desmosomes connecting cells

Functions: Provides strength and flexibility

Quiz: Which feature helps cells stick together?

Stratum Basale ▼

Stage in shedding: Deepest layer, cells divide continuously.

Structures: Basal keratinocytes, melanocytes

Functions: Regeneration of epidermis, pigment production

Quiz: True or False: This layer regenerates the skin.

Dermis ▼

Papillary Layer

Structures: Capillaries, sensory receptors

Functions: Nourishes epidermis, sensation

Reticular Layer

Structures: Collagen fibers, sweat glands, hair follicles

Functions: Strength, elasticity, thermoregulation

Quiz: Which dermis layer provides elasticity?

Subcutaneous Layer ▼

Structures

  • Adipose tissue (fat)
  • Blood vessels

Functions

  • Insulation
  • Energy storage
  • Anchors skin to underlying tissues

Quiz: True or False: Subcutaneous layer stores fat.
